How to Install
---------------------
1. Go to the following site: http://compsci.ca/holtsoft/ then download Turing 4.1.1 for Windows onto your desktop.
2. Once download is complete, extract the .zip file using WinZIP into your documents folder.
3. Right-click Turing.exe then create shortcut and drag the new shortcut to your desktop.

---------------------
How to Play
---------------------

1. Insert the Canadian North Rescue CD into your CD-ROM drive.
2. Click on the Turing shortcut you just created. The Turing windows should now appear.
3. In the window's top menu, click on Open. Browse to the CD's contents and find CanadianNorthRescue.t. The coding for the game should display in the Turing window.
4. Click Run.

F.A.Q.
---------------------

Q: Why does he have to look for paw prints?
A: He has to find his pet bear. Wouldn't you do the same?

Q. What does the maple leaf/Tim Hortons/bacon/poutine/pancakes do?
A: Didn't you read the instructions? That screen will tell you what does what.

Q: What happens when Matt's health is 0?
A: You'd rather not know. But you basically lose and have to start over.

Q: Can I save so that I can continue on a level I stopped at?
A: No! That's too easy!

Q: Can I use both the maple leaf and the Tim Hortons cup at the same time?
A: Yes, of course! Speed and invincibility is the best way to go!

Q: How about using two maple leafs, or two Tim Hortons cups?
A: No; once you use one, you can't use another one until the first one is finished.

Q: Are the Russians really invading Canada?
A: No... but they did plant their Russian flag onto the North Pole, which is basically on Canada's borderline. It's an Arctic chase for new resources and minerals, involving not only Russia and Canada but the United States, Norway and Denmark. You can find more information in this article: http://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/national/canada-pushes-past-north-pole-in-arctic-survey/article1151195/

Q: I never get a mini-game!
A: That's not a question...
